The Candida Protocol & The Candida Connection

What is Candida?

Candida is basically just yeast or fungus. In an ideal world we would have a little bit always working with the body. The problem is, especially in Western culture, we eat lots of pseudo-food, take lots of medication and stress about EVERYTHING.

This creates the perfect environment for candida to thrive and grow beyond what it should.

What It Does

Candida in essence punctures holes through the gut, creating intolerances and systemic inflammation and infection. Once the gut is leaky - from the little holes being poked through by the candida - things that we eat and pathogens are able to escape into the system. This is when intolerances and allergies begin to happen, as the body gets busy mounting an immune reaction to all the little bits that escape.

My mentor Charles once told me that he never found a cancer that wasn't surrounded by candida. So, we must heal the gut and stop the candida. - Kelly Wotherspoon, Naturopath

What Triggers It

Candida overgrowth doesn't happen in a vacuum. There are clear triggers that tip the balance:

Antibiotics

Antibiotics kill bacteria indiscriminately - including the protective Bifidobacterium and Lactobacillus species that keep candida in check. A single course of broad-spectrum antibiotics can wipe out colonisation resistance, giving candida an open field. Multiple courses compound the damage.

Acute Stress & Trauma

Acute stress and trauma activate the fight-or-flight response, flooding the body with cortisol. Cortisol suppresses immune surveillance (especially secretory IgA in the gut), increases intestinal permeability within hours, and shifts the microbiome toward pathogenic organisms. A single traumatic event can be the tipping point that allows candida to take hold. The body remembers - and stays locked in a stress state that feeds the overgrowth.

COVID-19 / Spike Protein

The spike protein directly damages intestinal epithelial cells and depletes Bifidobacterium - the primary organism that keeps candida contained. This is covered in detail in the research section below.

Mother to Baby Transmission

Candida is passed from mother to baby through breast milk. If the mother has systemic candida, the baby is colonised from birth. This means the child grows up with candida already established in their system, often presenting as colic, reflux, eczema, ear infections, and recurrent thrush. It's not genetic - it's transmitted. And it's treatable in both mother and child.

Mucous Membrane Dysfunction

At its core, systemic candida is mucous membrane dysfunction. Candida colonises mucous membranes throughout the entire body - gut, sinuses, lungs, ears, urinary tract, reproductive system, skin. Once the membranes are compromised, they become the breeding ground for everything:

  • Allergies - compromised nasal and respiratory mucosa overreacts to environmental antigens
  • Recurrent ear infections - candida in the Eustachian tube and middle ear mucosa
  • Skin conditions - eczema, acne, psoriasis, dermatitis (the skin is the body's largest mucous membrane)
  • Immune deficiency - 80% of immune tissue sits in the gut mucosa (GALT). Damage it, and systemic immunity collapses
  • Parasites & worms - a damaged, inflamed gut with compromised mucosa and suppressed immunity creates the ideal environment for parasitic colonisation. The candida opens the door, and everything else walks through
  • Recurrent UTIs & thrush - urogenital mucosa colonised by candida
  • Sinus infections - nasal and sinus mucosa compromised and chronically inflamed

This is why treating individual symptoms never works long-term. The antihistamines, the steroid creams, the antifungal pessaries, the antibiotics for ear infections - they address the surface while the underlying mucous membrane dysfunction persists. Fix the candida, restore the membranes, and the body stops producing symptoms because it no longer needs to.

The Immune System Goes Haywire

Once candida is systemic, three things happen simultaneously:

Autoimmune Disease

The immune system produces Th17 cells to fight candida. Published in Cell journal: these Th17 cells cross-react with human tissue through molecular mimicry. Your body's defence against the fungus starts attacking your own organs - thyroid (Hashimotos), joints (RA), nervous system (MS), gut (Crohns, UC).

Shao, T. et al. (2019) "Human Anti-fungal Th17 Immunity and Pathology Rely on Cross-Reactivity against Candida albicans." Cell, 176(6)

MCAS (Mast Cell Activation Syndrome)

Candida glycoprotein directly activates mast cells, triggering degranulation and histamine release. With 80% of immune tissue in the gut, and the gut full of candida, the mast cells are in a state of constant activation. Histamine floods the system causing hives, flushing, brain fog, anxiety, heart palpitations, and food reactions.

Lopes, J. et al. (2012) "Role and Relevance of Mast Cells in Fungal Infections." Clinical and Developmental Immunology

POTS (Postural Orthostatic Tachycardia Syndrome)

Candida in the gut irritates the vagus nerve - the major nerve connecting the gut to the brain and heart. Vagal irritation combined with mast cell-driven inflammation disrupts the autonomic nervous system. Heart rate spikes on standing, blood pressure drops, dizziness, fainting - the body's thermostat is broken.

Candida drives insulin resistance (via systemic inflammation), which increases IGF-1 and androgens, which increases sebum production. Gut dysbiosis is 10x more prevalent in acne patients. The gut-brain-skin theory (Stokes & Pillsbury, 1930) - now validated by modern research - identified this connection nearly a century ago.

Urticaria / Hives

Candida directly activates mast cells in the skin. Combined with histamine intolerance from DAO enzyme suppression (candida metabolites impair DAO function), the result is chronic hives, flushing, and itching that antihistamines alone cannot resolve.

The Body Holds the Score - Nervous System & Somatic Connection

Candida doesn't just affect biochemistry - it locks the entire body into a stress state. And that stress state feeds the candida right back. This is the feedback loop that keeps people stuck:

The Candida-Stress-Body Loop

Candida drives constant immune activation, producing inflammatory cytokines (IL-6, TNF-alpha) that signal danger to the nervous system. The body interprets this as threat and shifts into fight-or-flight (sympathetic dominance).

When the nervous system is locked in fight-or-flight, the psoas muscle - the only muscle connecting your spine to your legs - contracts and stays contracted. This is a primal protective reflex. But chronic psoas contraction:

  • Compresses the diaphragm - restricting breathing to shallow chest breaths
  • Reduces vagal tone - the vagus nerve runs through the diaphragm, and restricted breathing means reduced vagal stimulation
  • Drops parasympathetic function - the body cannot rest, digest, or heal
  • Compresses the gut - physically restricting blood flow and motility in the intestines
  • Traps trauma in the fascia - fascia contains approximately 250 million nerve endings and acts as a sensory organ, holding stress patterns in the body

The result: gut motility slows, candida thrives in the stagnant environment, immune surveillance drops further, and the loop perpetuates.

Modern Life Makes It Worse

We sit for 8-12 hours a day. Sitting shortens the psoas, compresses the gut, and reduces diaphragmatic breathing - keeping the nervous system in a low-grade stress state even without psychological stress. Add processed food, antibiotic history, and the post-COVID microbiome damage, and you have the perfect storm: a body locked in fight-or-flight, with a gut full of candida, and a nervous system that cannot access the parasympathetic state needed to heal.

Candida and Cancer

Candida produces acetaldehyde - classified by the International Agency for Research on Cancer (IARC) as a Group 1 carcinogen (the same classification as asbestos and tobacco smoke). Through biofilm formation, candida ensures long-term tissue exposure to this carcinogen.

Published mechanisms include: activation of oncogenic signalling pathways, chronic NF-kB inflammation (the master switch for cancer progression), immune suppression that allows abnormal cells to evade detection, and direct tumour promotion via hypoxia signalling.

Environmental cancers - those not caused by inherited genetic mutations (BRCA, Li-Fraumeni, Lynch syndrome) - have candida creating the conditions: chronic inflammation, carcinogenic metabolites, immune evasion, and a leaky gut allowing other carcinogens systemic access.
